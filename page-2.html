Effects of NAFTA and Immigration

Interviews conducted by students

December 6, 2018 Edith Ordonez,
Noely Caballero

One of the economic effects of immigration has definitely cheap labor. People who have immigrated to the United states choose to immigrate because the effects of the Free Trade Agreement offered more jobs that come with poor working conditions. Not only did NAFTA create jobs but it also caused 1.3 million Mexican farmers to abandon their plots.

WHAT HAPPENED IN 1980, EL SALVADOR?

DECEMBER 3, 2018 GIANNI MADERA,
FREDI JUAREZ

In 1980, A civil war had commenced in El Salvador. The civil war and subsequent mass migration within El Salvador occured due to the effort to spread capitalism by force. As a result of the clash between economic systems and the military-led government failing to fulfill promises (such as improving living conditions), there followed the formation of the Farabundo Marti National Liberation Front (FMLN). El Salvador’s military government suppressed socialist groups, such as the FMLN with aid from the U.S. in ways that were inhumane.
